# Pizza glossary — every term you'll encounter

A reference for the vocabulary you'll see in recipes, restaurant menus, and
pizza forums. Each entry is brief but precise.

## Style names

- **Apizza** (ah-BEETZ): the New Haven, CT regional pronunciation of
  "pizza." Refers specifically to the New Haven coal-oven-style pizza
  served at Frank Pepe's, Sally's, and Modern Apizza.

- **Bianca** (Italian: "white"): a pizza without tomato sauce. Typically
  has olive oil, garlic, herbs, often ricotta or mozzarella.

- **Calzone**: a folded pizza, sealed at the edges, baked. Filled with
  ricotta, mozzarella, optional cured meat.

- **Fugazza** / **Fugazzeta**: an Argentine pizza style — thick, focaccia-
  base, topped with mozzarella and a huge amount of caramelised onion.

- **Margherita**: pizza with tomato, mozzarella (typically fresh), basil,
  olive oil. The canonical Neapolitan.

- **Marinara**: pizza with tomato, garlic, oregano, olive oil, no cheese.
  Older than Margherita.

- **Pinsa**: a Roman-style oval pizza made from a high-hydration dough
  with a mix of wheat, rice, and soy flour. Marketed as "more digestible."
  A 21st-century invention (named "pinsa" for marketing reasons), not an
  ancient tradition.

- **Pizza al taglio**: Rome's "by the cut" pizza, sold by weight from
  long rectangular trays.

- **Pizza al metro**: a long Neapolitan-area pizza served by the meter.

- **Pizza fritta**: deep-fried Neapolitan dough, filled or topped after
  frying.

- **Sfincione**: the original Sicilian pizza, with onions, anchovies,
  oregano, breadcrumbs, no mozzarella. Different from American
  "Sicilian-style."

- **Stuffed pizza**: a Chicago variant of deep-dish, with an additional
  layer of cheese on top of the toppings, capped with a thin layer of
  dough before sauce.

## Dough and technique terms

- **Autolyse**: a 20-60 minute rest period after mixing only flour and
  water (no salt, no yeast). Allows flour to fully hydrate and gluten to
  start forming on its own.

- **Baker's percentage**: the convention of expressing ingredients as a
  percentage of flour weight. Flour is always 100%.

- **Biga**: an Italian pre-ferment, low-hydration (~50-55%), used for
  flavour and structure. See yeast-and-preferments.md.

- **Bulk fermentation**: the first rise of the full dough mass, before
  dividing into individual portions.

- **Cold ferment**: refrigerator-temperature fermentation (1-3°C / 33-37°F)
  lasting 24-72 hours. The single biggest flavour upgrade for home pizza.

- **Crumb**: the interior structure of the baked dough. An "open crumb"
  has large, irregular alveoli (holes). A "tight crumb" has small,
  uniform ones.

- **Diastatic malt powder**: a flour additive containing amylase enzymes
  that break starch into sugars during fermentation. Helps browning at
  lower oven temperatures.

- **Dough ball** (or "dough portion"): an individual pizza-sized portion
  of dough (typically 200-300g for Neapolitan, 400-500g for NY).

- **Extensibility**: a dough's willingness to stretch without springing
  back. Develops with longer fermentation.

- **Gluten**: the protein network that forms when wheat flour is hydrated
  and worked. Provides structure and elasticity. Gluten develops by
  kneading, by stretch-and-fold, or by time alone.

- **Hydration**: water as a percentage of flour weight. Low hydration =
  dense, dry dough; high hydration = open, airy crumb, hard to handle.

- **Levain** (French) / **lievito madre** (Italian): sourdough starter.

- **Poolish**: a French pre-ferment, 100% hydration, used for flavour.

- **Stretch-and-fold**: a technique for developing gluten without
  kneading, by folding the dough over itself at intervals. Used for
  high-hydration doughs.

- **Windowpane test**: a check for gluten development. Stretch a small
  piece of dough thin — if it's translucent without tearing, gluten is
  fully developed.

## Crust and topping terms

- **Cornicione** (cor-nee-CHO-ne): the puffed rim of a Neapolitan-style
  pizza. The signature visual element of the style.

- **Crown**: another name for the crust rim, especially in American
  contexts.

- **Frico**: the crispy lacy edge of cheese caramelised against a Detroit-
  style pan. The literal definition is "anything fried" — applies in
  cheese contexts to the edge cheese specifically.

- **Leopard spots**: the small distinct black char marks on a Neapolitan
  cornicione. Sign of a properly cooked pie at 480°C.

- **Maillard browning**: the chemical browning reaction that gives crust
  its golden colour and rich flavour. Happens above ~140°C / 285°F.

- **Crumb shot**: a photo of the cross-section of a crust, revealing the
  interior structure. A "good crumb shot" is bragging rights on pizza
  forums.

- **Bake** (verb / noun): the process of putting a pizza in the oven, or
  the resulting cooked pizza. "Great bake on this one."

- **Cup-and-char** (pepperoni): the curling and charring behaviour of
  certain small-diameter pepperoni varieties, creating bowl-shaped slices
  filled with rendered fat.

## Equipment terms

- **Baking steel**: a thick steel plate that sits in your oven, absorbs
  heat, and transfers it to the bottom of the pizza. Replaces ceramic
  baking stones.

- **Bench**: the work surface for handling dough. Also a "bench scraper"
  is the small metal scraper for dough work.

- **Deck oven**: a commercial pizza oven with horizontal "decks" of stone
  on which pizzas bake. The traditional NY pizzeria oven.

- **Dough box**: a flat plastic container with a lid, used to ferment
  multiple dough balls separated and covered.

- **Lloyd Pans**: the standard brand of Detroit-style pizza pan in the
  US.

- **Peel**: the flat paddle used to launch pizzas into hot ovens and
  retrieve them. Wood peels for launching; metal peels for retrieving.

- **Pizza stone**: a ceramic disc or rectangle for baking pizza on.
  Largely superseded by baking steels for home use.

- **Screen**: a perforated metal disc that holds pizza shape during
  baking. Used by some pizzerias for high-hydration doughs.

- **Turning peel**: a small round peel used inside hot ovens to rotate
  pizzas without removing them.

## Cheese terms

- **Brick cheese**: a Wisconsin cheese specific to Detroit-style pizza.
  Tangy, high-fat, browns well.

- **Burrata**: a fresh cheese with a mozzarella shell and a creamy
  interior of stracciatella. Added after the bake.

- **Caciocavallo**: a southern Italian cheese, semi-firm, sometimes used
  on sfincione and other regional pies.

- **Fior di latte**: cow's-milk fresh mozzarella, traditional on
  Neapolitan Margherita.

- **Galbani / Polly-O / Grande**: brand names for low-moisture mozzarella
  widely used in American pizzerias.

- **Low-moisture mozzarella**: the dry, shreddable mozzarella used on NY
  and most American-style pizzas. Does not mean low-fat.

- **Mozzarella di bufala**: water-buffalo mozzarella, richer and wetter
  than cow's-milk fresh mozz. The most traditional Neapolitan cheese.

- **Pecorino Romano**: a sharp, salty sheep's-milk grating cheese.
  Common as a finishing dust on many styles.

- **Provel**: a processed cheese specific to St. Louis-style pizza.
  Blend of cheddar, Swiss, and provolone.

- **Stracciatella**: the soft, milky interior of burrata. Sometimes
  served on its own as a topping.

## Sauce and topping terms

- **Crushed tomatoes**: tomatoes that have been milled with skins on,
  resulting in a chunky thick sauce.

- **Passata**: pureed tomato sauce, smooth, often passed through a sieve.
  Thinner than crushed tomatoes.

- **Pomodoro**: simply "tomato" in Italian. "Pizza al pomodoro" is a
  marinara-style pie.

- **San Marzano**: a specific variety of tomato grown near Naples. The
  AVPN-spec San Marzano DOP carries a Consorzio number.

- **DOP** (Denominazione di Origine Protetta): the Italian protected-
  origin designation for products like San Marzano DOP and Mozzarella di
  Bufala Campana DOP.

## Restaurant and culture terms

- **AVPN**: Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana — the organisation that
  certifies authentic Neapolitan pizzerias according to strict criteria.

- **Pizzaiolo**: a pizza-maker.

- **Pizzeria**: a pizza shop.

- **Slice shop**: an American (especially NY) pizzeria selling individual
  slices, usually reheated on demand from large pre-baked pies.

- **Party cut** / **tavern cut**: cutting a round pizza into small
  squares instead of triangles. The Chicago thin-crust standard.

- **18-incher**: a standard large NY pie.

- **Eighth**: a single slice of a NY pizza (cut into 8). Different from a
  "slice" in different pizzerias, but generally what you ask for at a
  slice shop.

## Acronyms and shorthand

- **NY-style**: New York-style.
- **DOP**: see above.
- **00 flour**: a finely-ground Italian flour, type 00 (also written
  "doppio zero").
- **APN**: short for AVPN.
- **CSS**: not a pizza term (this is a glossary, not a coding doc).

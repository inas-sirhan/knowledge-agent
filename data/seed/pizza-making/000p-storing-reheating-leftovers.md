# Storing, reheating, and using leftover pizza and dough

Pizza is delicious fresh out of the oven, but leftover pizza done right is
arguably better than mediocre fresh pizza. This document covers how to
store, freeze, and reheat both finished pizzas and unused dough.

## Storing leftover pizza

### Same-day refrigeration

- Pizza cooled to room temperature in 1-2 hours: refrigerate in an
  airtight container or wrapped in plastic. Lasts 3-4 days.
- Don't stack slices directly on top of each other — they'll stick. Use
  parchment paper between layers, or a single-layer container.

### Freezing finished pizza

- Wrap individual slices in plastic wrap, then again in foil. Lasts 2-3
  months in the freezer without significant quality loss.
- For best results, freeze on a sheet pan first (uncovered, 30 min) so
  the toppings firm up — then wrap. This prevents the cheese from
  sticking to the wrap and tearing off when you unwrap.

## Reheating — by the slice

The right method depends on what you have.

### Cast-iron skillet (best home method)

1. Heat a dry skillet over medium heat for 2 minutes.
2. Place the slice in. Cover with a lid (this is the key step — captures
   steam to re-melt cheese without drying out).
3. After 3-4 minutes, the bottom is crisp and the cheese is melted again.
4. Optional: add 2 drops of water to the pan before covering — the steam
   re-softens the cheese without making the slice soggy.

The cast-iron method makes a 2-day-old slice taste better than fresh
delivery pizza.

### Toaster oven

- 400°F / 200°C, 5-7 minutes on a tray (not on a wire rack — drips of
  cheese will fall and burn).
- A standalone toaster oven is one of the most pizza-friendly small
  appliances you can own.

### Conventional oven

- 425°F / 220°C, 5-7 minutes directly on a rack (place foil below to
  catch drips).
- The "directly on the rack" placement gets the bottom crisp.

### Air fryer

- 375°F / 190°C, 3-4 minutes. Great results, possibly the easiest method.

### What NOT to use

- **Microwave**. Cheese goes rubbery, crust goes soggy. Microwaved pizza
  is sad pizza.
- **Hot toaster slot**. Don't try to toast a slice — the cheese melts down
  into the elements and your toaster smokes for a week.

## Cold pizza for breakfast

A respected tradition. The crust hardens slightly overnight, the cheese
firms up, and the flavours meld. Some people genuinely prefer this. No
reheating required. Whether you do this is a matter of cultural identity.

## Storing leftover dough

### Refrigerator (1-3 days)

- Dough that's been balled and cold-fermented can stay in the fridge for
  up to 5 days total (e.g., your 72-hour ferment plan + 2 more days as
  buffer). Beyond that, gluten breaks down and the dough goes flat.
- Signs of over-ferment: dough is sticky, smells alcoholic, won't hold
  shape.

### Freezing dough

- After bulk fermentation and balling, lightly oil each ball and place
  in a zip-top freezer bag. Press out air, seal, freeze flat (so they
  stack).
- Frozen dough lasts ~2 months. Quality degrades slowly past that.
- To use: move from freezer to fridge 24 hours before you want to bake.
  Then pull from fridge 2 hours before stretching (same as a fresh cold-
  ferment dough).

### When freezing doesn't work as well

- Doughs with sourdough starter freeze acceptably but lose some character.
- Very high-hydration doughs (Roman al taglio at 80%+) freeze and thaw
  less gracefully than 60-65% NY-style doughs.

## What to do with leftover dough scraps

After stretching your pizza, you'll have small dough trimmings (especially
with Roman al taglio or grandma). Don't throw them out.

### Garlic knots

1. Roll trimmings into small ropes (~10 cm long, 1 cm thick).
2. Tie each rope into a single knot.
3. Place on a sheet pan, proof 30 min, brush with melted butter mixed
   with minced garlic, salt, and parsley.
4. Bake at 475°F for 10-12 min.
5. Brush again with the garlic butter immediately out of the oven.

The pizzeria classic. Better than the pizza itself for many people.

### Focaccia chips

1. Roll trimmings paper-thin with a rolling pin.
2. Brush with olive oil, sprinkle salt and rosemary or dried oregano.
3. Bake at 500°F for 5-7 min until crisp and golden.
4. Eat with antipasti or as crackers.

### Bruschetta base

1. Roll out small rounds.
2. Bake at 500°F for 4 min until crisp.
3. Top with chopped tomatoes + garlic + basil + olive oil.

## What to do with leftover sauce

- Refrigerates for 5-7 days.
- Freezes well — portion into ice cube trays, then transfer cubes to a
  freezer bag. Each cube is roughly one tablespoon — convenient for
  small applications later.
- Use in: pasta sauce (just heat with a little olive oil and basil),
  shakshuka, on toast with cheese melted under the broiler, as a dipping
  sauce for breadsticks.

## What to do with leftover toppings

- **Pepperoni**: keeps 2-3 weeks unopened, 1 week after opening.
- **Mozzarella**: low-moisture keeps 1-2 weeks; fresh mozz, 5 days.
- **Sausage**: cook it all and freeze the cooked excess.
- **Vegetables**: use within 3 days for raw, 5 days for cooked.

A common move: any topping you have leftover can go into a frittata or
omelette the next morning. Pepperoni and onion frittata is excellent.

## Reheating a whole pizza (not by the slice)

If you have a whole leftover pizza:

- 425°F / 220°C, on a baking steel or stone, **directly on the steel**.
- 6-10 minutes total.
- The steel does to a leftover pizza what it does to fresh — crisps the
  bottom while gently warming the top.

## A specific tip: par-bake and reheat for hosting

For a dinner party, par-bake all your pizzas in advance, freeze, then
finish-bake when guests are over. This is what some pizzerias do for slice
shops. The technique:

1. Make and stretch dough.
2. Top **only** with sauce.
3. Par-bake 3-4 minutes (longer than you might think — get the bottom set).
4. Cool, wrap individually, freeze.
5. Day of: top par-baked frozen pies with cheese + toppings, bake at
   500°F directly on the steel for 5-6 minutes.

This is the trick that lets you serve "5 fresh pizzas in 30 minutes"
without your kitchen being a war zone.

## A piece of advice

Pizza is a freezer-friendly food. Make extra dough, freeze unused balls.
Make sauce in bigger batches than you need. Par-bake during a quiet
afternoon for a future busy weeknight. The 30-minute pizza night becomes
a 12-minute pizza night when you've planned ahead.

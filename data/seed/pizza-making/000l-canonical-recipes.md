# Canonical recipes — six complete pizzas you can actually make

Each recipe below is a complete walkthrough: dough → sauce → assembly →
bake. Quantities are scaled for **4 pizzas** unless noted, so you can do a
proper pizza night for a family. Each recipe specifies the home-oven
adaptation alongside the canonical version.

## 1. Margherita (Neapolitan)

The original. Demands the right ingredients but the recipe is short.

### Dough (4 pies, ~250g each)

```
600  g  type 00 flour (Caputo Pizzeria or equivalent)
390  g  water (65% hydration)
 17  g  fine sea salt (2.8%)
   1 g  fresh yeast (or 0.3 g instant)
```

1. Dissolve salt in water. Whisk in ~70% of flour to a slurry. Add yeast.
   Mix in the remaining flour.
2. Knead 12-15 min until smooth and elastic.
3. Bulk ferment 2 hr at room temp.
4. Divide into 4 × 250 g balls. Place in a sealed dough box, well-spaced.
5. **Cold ferment 18-24 hr** in the fridge.
6. Pull from fridge 2 hr before baking.

### Sauce

```
1 can quality whole peeled tomatoes (San Marzano or equivalent)
1 tsp sea salt
```

Hand-crush. Add salt. Done.

### Cheese & toppings (per pie)

- 80-100 g fior di latte mozzarella, torn into 2 cm pieces, drained for
  20 min before use.
- 4-5 fresh basil leaves.
- Drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil after bake.
- Tiny pinch of salt on top before bake.

### Assembly & bake

1. Stretch one dough ball to ~30 cm by hand. Leave a 2 cm rim.
2. Top: 3 tbsp sauce in centre, spread thin with the back of a spoon.
   Distribute cheese in the middle (not to the edge). Salt lightly.
3. **Outdoor pizza oven**: 450°C / 850°F floor, 60-90 sec, rotating once.
4. **Home oven (550°F max)**: preheat steel 60 min on upper rack; broil
   last 10 min; bake 3-4 min until edges puff and char.
5. Add basil and oil after the bake.

## 2. NY pepperoni

### Dough (4 pies, ~430g each = 14-inch pies)

```
1000 g  bread flour or high-gluten flour
 620 g  water (62%)
  25 g  salt (2.5%)
   3 g  instant yeast (0.3%)
  25 g  sugar (2.5%)
  25 g  olive oil (2.5%)
```

1. Mix water + sugar + yeast + salt. Add flour and oil. Mix to shaggy.
2. Rest 20 min. Knead 10 min.
3. Divide into 4 × 430 g balls.
4. Cold ferment **48-72 hr**.
5. Pull out 2 hr before baking.

### Sauce

```
1 can (28 oz) whole peeled tomatoes
2 cloves garlic, grated
1 tsp dried oregano
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
2 tbsp olive oil
Pinch red pepper flakes (optional)
```

Simmer 15-20 min uncovered, reduce thick. Cool.

### Toppings (per pie)

- 80-100 g sauce
- 150-180 g low-moisture mozzarella, freshly shredded
- 50-60 g cup-and-char pepperoni (small-cup, like Vermont Smoke and Cure)

### Assembly & bake

1. Stretch dough to 14 inches. Place on a semolina-dusted peel.
2. Sauce edge-to-edge with 1 cm border.
3. Cheese over sauce. Pepperoni on cheese.
4. **Home oven**: launch onto preheated baking steel at 550°F upper rack.
   Bake 6-8 min, rotating once. Switch to broil for the last 60-90 sec
   to brown cheese.
5. Rest 2 min before slicing.

## 3. Detroit pepperoni

### Dough (1 pan, 10×14)

```
500 g  bread flour
350 g  water (70%)
 12 g  salt
  3 g  instant yeast
 25 g  olive oil
```

1. Combine. Mix to shaggy. Rest 20 min. Knead 5 min.
2. Bulk 60 min at room temp.
3. Optional **24-hr cold ferment** for flavour (much better).
4. Coat 10×14 Detroit pan with 3 tbsp olive oil. Plop dough in.
5. Press toward edges with oiled hands. Cover, proof 60-90 min, gently
   re-pressing toward corners every 30 min.

### Sauce

```
1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
3 tbsp tomato paste
1 tbsp sugar
1 tbsp olive oil
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
1 tsp salt
```

Simmer 20 min uncovered. Cool. **Apply AFTER baking.**

### Cheese & toppings

- 280-340 g brick cheese cubes (or 50/50 brick + low-moisture mozz)
- 60 g cup-and-char pepperoni (optional, on dough first)

### Assembly & bake

1. Lay pepperoni directly on the proofed dough.
2. Distribute cheese cubes, **all the way to pan edges** (the edge cheese
   becomes the frico).
3. Bake at 500°F, lower-middle rack, 13-17 min until corners are deep
   caramel brown.
4. Out of the oven, immediately stripe sauce on top (3 stripes is
   classic).
5. Rest 5 min so the frico sets. Run a thin spatula around the edge.
   Carefully lift out and cut into squares.

## 4. Roman al taglio with potato & rosemary

### Dough (1 half-sheet pan)

```
500 g  bread flour (or 50/50 bread + 00)
400 g  water (80%)
 12 g  salt
  2 g  instant yeast
 25 g  olive oil
```

1. Combine, mix shaggy. Rest 20 min.
2. **4 stretch-and-folds** at 30-min intervals (no kneading).
3. **Cold ferment 48-72 hr.**
4. Day of: tip dough onto heavily semolina-dusted counter. Gently stretch
   into a rough rectangle.
5. Place onto heavily oiled half-sheet pan. Dimple with fingertips to
   fill the pan.
6. Proof 60-90 min at room temp.

### Topping (potato-rosemary)

- 2 medium yellow potatoes, sliced **paper-thin** (mandolin), soaked in
  cold water 10 min, drained, patted dry
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary
- Flaky sea salt

### Bake

1. Brush proofed dough with olive oil.
2. **Par-bake plain at 500°F** for 5 min.
3. Pull from oven. Layer potato slices in tight overlap, sprinkle rosemary
   and salt, drizzle more oil.
4. Return to oven. Bake 12-15 min more until potatoes are tender and
   edges of dough are golden.
5. Cool 5 min. Cut into rectangles with scissors or a pizza wheel.

## 5. Grandma pizza (with fresh tomato)

### Dough (1 sheet pan, 9×13)

```
400 g  bread flour
260 g  water (65%)
  8 g  salt
  3 g  instant yeast
 25 g  olive oil
```

1. Mix, knead 5 min, bulk 60 min.
2. Optional **24-hr cold ferment** (better).
3. Press into oiled 9×13 sheet pan.
4. Proof 60 min at room temp.

### Topping

- 1 pint cherry tomatoes (or 2 large ripe tomatoes, chopped), tossed
  with 1 tsp salt, 2 cloves grated garlic, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp dried
  oregano. Let sit 15 min.
- 150 g low-moisture mozzarella, shredded
- 2 tbsp grated Pecorino Romano
- Fresh basil after bake

### Bake

1. Top proofed dough: cheese first (a thin layer), then tomato mixture in
   small spoonfuls (not too wet — drain a little if it pooled).
2. Bake at 500°F, lower-middle rack, 15-18 min until bottom is browned
   and tomatoes have collapsed.
3. Pecorino dust + basil + olive oil drizzle after.
4. Cut into rectangles.

## 6. White pizza (bianca) — ricotta, garlic, parsley

A no-tomato classic.

### Dough

Use the NY recipe above. 4 dough balls, but you only need 1.

### Topping (per pie)

- 100 g whole-milk ricotta, drained
- 50 g low-moisture mozzarella, shredded
- 30 g Pecorino Romano, grated
- 2 cloves garlic confit (or roasted garlic), spread thin
- 2 tbsp olive oil + 1 clove minced raw garlic (mix together)
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley or basil, finely chopped, after bake
- Flaky sea salt

### Bake

1. Stretch dough to 12 inches.
2. Brush with garlic oil. Dot ricotta in small spoonfuls (don't spread
   continuously — they should stay distinct).
3. Sprinkle mozzarella between ricotta dollops. Sprinkle Pecorino over
   everything.
4. Bake on steel at 550°F for 6-7 min until cheese is bubbling and edges
   are deeply browned.
5. Parsley + olive oil + flaky salt after the bake.

## Hot-honey pepperoni (modern American classic) — variation on NY pepperoni

Make the NY pepperoni recipe above. After bake, drizzle generously with
hot honey (Mike's Hot Honey or homemade). The combination of crispy
cup-and-char pepperoni, salty cheese, and sweet-heat honey is one of the
defining American pizza inventions of the last decade. Worth knowing.

## Calibrating to your kit

Every home oven is different. Adjustments to make on your first attempt:

- **Watch the colour** of the cornicione, not the clock.
- **Underbake the first one** by 30 sec to see how your bottom does.
- **Try 1 minute under broil** at the end for cheese — but watch closely.
  Cheese goes from golden to charcoal in 90 seconds.
- **Take notes** after each pizza: oven temp, rack height, preheat
  duration, total bake time. After 3-4 pies you'll know your oven.

The single biggest piece of advice for any of these recipes: **don't
skip the long cold ferment**. The flavour difference is enormous and
costs nothing but time.

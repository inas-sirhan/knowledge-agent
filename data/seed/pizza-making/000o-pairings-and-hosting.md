# Pairings and hosting — wine, beer, sides, and how to host a pizza night

The pizza is the main event but the night around it is what people
remember. This document covers drinks, sides, and the logistics of feeding
a group good pizza.

## Drinks

### Wine pairings

Pizza is famously wine-friendly. The fundamental match:

- **Red, medium-bodied, high-acid** → cuts the cheese richness, complements
  tomato. **Italian reds** are the natural fit: Chianti, Barbera,
  Montepulciano d'Abruzzo, Nero d'Avola.
- For **white pizzas** (no tomato): **whites with weight** — Greco di Tufo,
  Vermentino, a buttery Chardonnay.
- For **Neapolitan Margherita**: a young, vibrant Chianti, or a Aglianico
  for more body.
- For **NY pepperoni**: a Zinfandel or a fruitier Sangiovese — the fruit
  matches the spice.
- For **Detroit**: anything with backbone — Nebbiolo, big Chianti
  Classico Riserva, or a structured Zin.
- For **white clam apizza** (New Haven): crisp dry white — Vermentino,
  Albariño, a good Pinot Grigio.

A few rules:

- **Avoid heavy oak**. Oaky Chardonnays clash with tomato.
- **Avoid heavy tannin**. Big Cabernets are pizza killers — the tannin
  fights the cheese.
- **Sparkling reds** (Lambrusco) are an under-appreciated pizza match.
  The bubbles cut richness; the moderate alcohol means dinner doesn't
  end early.

### Beer pairings

The natural drink for casual pizza nights:

- **Pilsner** (Italian or Czech): clean, dry, refreshing. Goes with
  everything.
- **Pale ale / IPA**: matches NY pizza's salt and fat. Avoid very bitter
  hoppy IPAs that fight the tomato.
- **Saison**: peppery, dry, surprising match for white pizzas.
- **Belgian Tripel**: rich enough for Detroit's frico.
- **Lambic** (Gueuze): surprising match for tomato-heavy pies. The acidity
  echoes the sauce.

A great default for hosting: **Pilsner Urquell or Birra Moretti**. Cheap,
universally pleasant, doesn't compete with the pizza.

### Non-alcoholic

- **San Pellegrino** (sparkling water + a slice of lemon).
- **Italian sodas**: aranciata, chinotto.
- **Coca-Cola** is unironically a great pizza drink. Don't fight it.

## Sides

A pizza night with **just pizza** is fine but feels one-note. A few
strategic sides:

### Arugula salad with lemon and olive oil

The classic. Sharp, peppery arugula tossed with lemon juice, good olive
oil, salt, and shaved parmigiano. Cuts pizza richness. Can also be served
**on top of a hot pizza** (it wilts slightly — delicious).

```
4 cups arugula
juice of 1/2 lemon
2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
salt, pepper
shaved parmigiano
```

Toss right before serving.

### Antipasto plate

Cured meats (prosciutto, mortadella, salame), cheeses (mozzarella balls,
parmigiano chunks), olives, marinated artichokes, sun-dried tomatoes,
bread sticks. Lets people graze while pizzas come out one at a time.

### Caesar salad

Mainstream but it works. Use a Caesar dressing with anchovy if you make
it from scratch.

### Caprese

Tomato + fresh mozzarella + basil + olive oil + flaky salt. The Italian
canonical side. Easy when tomatoes are in season.

### Marinated white beans

Cannellini beans with garlic, olive oil, parsley, lemon zest. Surprisingly
satisfying side.

### Garlic knots

For an American pizza night, garlic knots are unbeatable. Roll dough
trimmings into knots, brush with melted butter + garlic + parsley, bake at
500°F for 8 min, brush again hot out of the oven.

### Desserts

- **Cannoli** if you can buy them fresh nearby.
- **Tiramisu** if you have time the day before.
- **Affogato**: a scoop of gelato or vanilla ice cream with a shot of
  espresso poured over it. 60-second dessert that crushes.
- **Fresh fruit + a quality olive oil + flaky salt**: works with stone
  fruit, strawberries, or melon. Surprising.

## Hosting timeline — for 6 people, 4 pizzas

**Day before:**

- Mix dough. Bulk ferment 2 hr. Portion into balls. Refrigerate.
- Make sauce. Refrigerate.
- Buy / prep all toppings.

**3 hours before guests arrive:**

- Pull dough balls from fridge.
- Pre-cook any toppings that need it (sausage, mushrooms, onions).
- Set up the toppings table: small bowls of each ingredient lined up.
- Wash and dress the salad greens (don't toss with dressing yet).

**1 hour before:**

- Preheat oven (steel inside) at max temp for 60 min.
- Or fire up the outdoor pizza oven.
- Open the first bottle of wine and let it breathe.

**As guests arrive:**

- Set out antipasto / starters.
- Pour drinks.

**Bake order** (for 4 pizzas + 6 people):

- One pizza at a time, but **stagger** so a fresh pie comes out every
  4-6 minutes.
- Cut each pizza into 8 slices.
- People take what they want; you bake the next one.
- This is much better than baking all 4 simultaneously (cold-by-the-time-
  you-sit-down pizza is the saddest pizza).

**For larger groups**: bake **sequentially, all night long**, never
trying to feed everyone at once. Pizza night has a slow rhythm. Lean
into it.

## Common hosting mistakes

- **All pizzas at once.** Either you bake them simultaneously (impossible
  in a home oven without crowding) or you bake them in sequence (the right
  way — but accept that dinner spans 30-60 minutes, not all served at one
  moment). Set the expectation.

- **Too many toppings options.** Five toppings is plenty. Ten makes you
  paralyzed about what to put on each pie. Decide in advance which 3-4
  pizzas you'll make.

- **No vegetarian option.** Always have at least one pizza without meat.
  A Margherita is a safe vegetarian + crowd-pleasing default.

- **All cheese-heavy pizzas.** Have one lighter pizza in the rotation —
  a marinara (no cheese), or a Margherita with light cheese, or a vegetable-
  topped Roman al taglio.

- **Running out of dough mid-way.** Make 6 dough balls when you have 6
  guests. Extra dough balls freeze well for next time.

- **Forgetting the kids / picky eaters.** Always have one "boring" pizza
  (plain cheese, plain pepperoni) for the kid who hates "weird stuff."

## A canonical 4-pizza menu for hosting

This menu covers different flavour profiles, has at least one
vegetarian option, and ranges in adventure level:

1. **Margherita** (Neapolitan or NY style). The safe crowd-pleaser.
2. **Pepperoni** (NY or Detroit). The American default.
3. **Mushroom + caramelised onion + thyme + ricotta** (white pizza). A
   restaurant-feeling vegetarian option.
4. **Hot honey pepperoni**. The modern showpiece.

Pair with: a bottle of Italian red, a bottle of crisp Italian white, a
few pilsners. Arugula salad. Cannoli or affogato for dessert.

## Solo pizza night

For a single eater:

- One dough ball → one personal-sized 8-inch pie. Use leftover dough for
  bar pies or garlic knots.
- Cast-iron skillet method: heat the skillet on the stovetop, lay
  stretched dough in, top, transfer to a 500°F oven for 8 min. Crisp
  bottom, hot top. A great single-person dinner.

## Pizza for a crowd (15+ people)

Make Roman al taglio. One half-sheet pan feeds 8-10 people, eats
beautifully at room temperature, and lets people serve themselves. Make
two pans with different toppings. Add a salad and bread. Done.
